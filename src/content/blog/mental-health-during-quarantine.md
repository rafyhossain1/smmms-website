The extended lockdown and quarantine periods presented unprecedented challenges for student mental health and wellbeing. Isolation from friends, disruption of routines, and uncertainty about the future created significant psychological stress for many young people. At SMMM School, we recognized the critical importance of addressing these concerns and providing support to help students navigate these difficult times.

Mental health is just as important as physical health, and students should know that seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. We encouraged students to maintain social connections through virtual study groups, online clubs, and regular communication with family and friends. Regular physical activity, even within home spaces, plays a vital role in managing stress and anxiety. Additionally, practicing mindfulness, journaling, and engaging in creative hobbies provides emotional outlets and helps process difficult feelings.

If you or someone you know is struggling with mental health during quarantine, reach out to school counselors, trusted teachers, or family members. Remember that challenges are temporary, and with proper support and self-care strategies, we can overcome them. Your wellbeing matters, and SMMM School is committed to supporting the holistic development of our students, including their mental and emotional health.
